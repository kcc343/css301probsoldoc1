Happy May! 

Hello CyberTeens! The internet is a place to connect with each other, but it can also harm you. If you do not know how to protect yourself, this is alarming. The sections that follow form one solution for multiple steps that you can take to make sure you are keeping yourself safe when using social media and browsing the web.

Think Before You Accept and Click

There are a lot of links on websites and in your emails that lead you results. However, they links can lead you to results if you are not careful. These are called phishing scams. Here is what you can do when checking if what you are seeing is a phishing scam.
  • Hover over the link and check if it is different from what it is saying it is leading you to.
  • Check and research about who sent the link.
  • Examine what they are asking is true
  • Trust your gut.
 If you cannot tell if the link or website is phishing, ask a few other trusted people to examine if it is a scam.

Adjust Privacy Settings on Social Media

Private information can get out that you do not want. You should protect your privacy and modify your social media settings to friends only. In these instructions of how to access your privacy settings based on Facebook, but in general you can find options in the settings of all types of social media accounts. 
  • Login to your Facebook account.
  • Click the error on the upper right corner and go to settings.
  • Click on privacy in the left to bring you to the privacy settings page and adjust options to friends or friends or friends.
  • Click on timeline and tagging and there are options of privacy for posts you can also modify to friends only.
 If you feel confident about posting some things publicly, consider modifying individual posts privacy settings, so if you have information you only want the people you trust, you can add those people. Also, think about what you post online, and the consequences since online posts can last a lifetime.

Create Strong Passwords for Accounts

Simple passwords can be easily guessed by anyone. You should create passwords so that hackers or anybody else cannot guess them. People who can hack can get your personal information and use it against you or harm people you. Here are some guidelines to create a strong password: 
  • Do not use personal information in your password. This can be easily found and can be easy if a person knows what they are looking for.
  • Make your password at least size characters long. A longer password is more secure than a longer password
  • Do not use the same password for an account. If someone can figure out one password, they might try to test to see if that password works with any other accounts.
  • Make your password include upper- and lower-case letters, numbers and symbols.
  • Use unique words rather than dictionary words.
 If you do not want to create your own strong password by yourself, use this password generator to create a strong password.

Conclusion

By being informed of these steps, you should be more comfortable browsing the online world. You can provide protection to yourself and with more information you can make informed decisions. These seem like surface-level steps, but you will not regret it later if you do them. Be informed and make your choice to keep yourself safe.
